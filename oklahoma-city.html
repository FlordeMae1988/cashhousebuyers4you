⚠ OKC market reality check

Oklahoma City's market has shifted in favor of buyers.

OKC, Edmond, Norman, Moore — across the metro, homes are sitting on the market significantly longer than they did in 2021–2022. Days on market are up sharply. Buyers are selective. Oklahoma City FAQ

How fast can you close on my OKC home?
We can close in as few as 10 days on a cash offer. Foreclosure situation with an Oklahoma County auction date approaching? We can move in 5–7 days when time is critical. Call (405) 518-8292 immediately if you're facing a deadline.
Are you compliant with Oklahoma SB 1075's wholesaler-disclosure requirements?
Yes. Oklahoma Senate Bill 1075 (effective November 1, 2025) requires real estate wholesalers to provide written disclosures before any agreement is signed, give homeowners a 48-hour cancellation window, hold earnest money in an Oklahoma FDIC-insured bank, and use a standardized cancellation notice from OREC. All of our OKC-area contracts are fully compliant with SB 1075.
My OKC home has foundation issues. Do you still buy it?
Yes. Oklahoma City sits on the same expansive clay soils that cause foundation movement across Tulsa. Many OKC homes with foundation issues are nearly impossible to sell traditionally — lenders won't finance them and retail buyers walk away. We buy OKC homes with foundation problems as-is, no repairs or engineering reports required from you.
Which OKC communities do you serve?
We buy throughout the Oklahoma City metro: Edmond, Norman, Moore, Midwest City, Yukon, Mustang, Bethany, Del City, The Village, Nichols Hills, Bricktown, and surrounding communities across Oklahoma, Cleveland, Canadian, Logan, Grady, McClain, and Pottawatomie counties.
Do I need to clean out or repair anything before closing?
No. We buy OKC homes in any condition — items left behind, code violations, deferred maintenance, storm damage, or anything else. Leave whatever you can't take and walk away. We handle everything after closing.
Can you stop a foreclosure auction in time?
Often yes — if you call us early enough. Oklahoma County foreclosure auctions can be stopped right up until the sale date if a buyer closes before then. Call (405) 518-8292 immediately with your auction date. We will tell you honestly within 24 hours whether we can make it happen.
