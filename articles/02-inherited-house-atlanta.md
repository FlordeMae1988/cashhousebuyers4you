# Selling an Inherited House in Atlanta's Slow Market — 2026 Guide

**Losing a parent is hard enough. Dealing with their Atlanta house in today's market makes it harder. If you're an heir or executor trying to figure out what to do with the family home, here's what you actually need to know — not the 2021 advice that doesn't apply anymore.**

## Why Inherited Properties Are Sitting Longer Than Ever

Here's the reality Atlanta families are facing in 2026: **inherited homes are sitting on the market dramatically longer than they used to.** And it's often worse for inherited properties than for owner-occupied homes. Here's why:

- **Inherited homes often need updating.** Parents or grandparents lived in them for decades — kitchens from 1995, original carpet, older HVAC systems. In today's picky-buyer market, that stuff kills sales.
- **Multiple heirs complicate timing.** Every decision needs agreement. That takes weeks. Meanwhile, the property sits, utilities keep running, insurance premiums pile up, property taxes accrue.
- **Out-of-state heirs can't manage showings.** If you're in Ohio and the house is in East Atlanta, coordinating viewings with a real estate agent across time zones is a nightmare.
- **Emotional attachment slows down pricing decisions.** Family members don't always agree on what the home is worth. When buyers have leverage like they do today, those disagreements mean missed opportunities.

Meanwhile, the estate continues to have expenses. A typical inherited Atlanta home that sits for 6 months costs the estate $12,000–$20,000 in carrying costs alone (mortgage, taxes, insurance, utilities, maintenance). That comes straight out of what heirs eventually split.

## First: The Probate Reality

If you haven't already, here's the Georgia probate process in 60 seconds:

1. **File the will** with the Probate Court in the county where the deceased lived
2. **Letters Testamentary issued** — the executor now has legal authority (30–60 days)
3. **Notice to creditors** — published for 4 weeks
4. **Debts paid, estate accounting filed**
5. **Order closing estate / distribution to heirs**

**Typical Atlanta probate timeline:** 4–8 months for simple estates. Longer for contested ones.

**The good news for sellers in a slow market:** In Georgia, an executor with Letters Testamentary can sign a purchase contract for the property — meaning we can get under contract during probate and close when probate finalizes. **This is especially valuable in today's market because you don't have to wait for probate AND then wait for a buyer.** We're already lined up. Sale closes the moment probate does.

## The Step-Up Basis — Don't Miss This

If you remember nothing else from this article, remember this: **you don't pay capital gains tax on the increase in value between when your parent bought the home and when they died.**

Example:
- Parent bought the house in 1985 for $75,000
- Died in 2026 when the house was worth $325,000
- Your cost basis = $325,000 (the value at death, not the original $75,000)
- If you sell for $310,000 (realistic in today's slow market), you actually have a $15,000 LOSS on paper
- No capital gains tax owed

**This is a massive benefit.** Don't blow it by selling without a date-of-death appraisal on file.

**To lock in the step-up basis:**
- Get a date-of-death appraisal from a licensed Georgia appraiser ($400–$800)
- Keep the death certificate
- Keep all probate documents

Without these, the IRS can challenge your basis calculation later.

## Dealing With Multiple Heirs in Today's Market

This is the hardest part of inherited property sales. Common scenarios:

**Scenario: Siblings disagree on price.**

In a hot 2021 market, you could list it and the market would decide. In 2026's slow market, if one sibling insists on a price the market won't pay, the property sits for a year.

**Solution:** Independent appraisal + our multi-option quote. We give everyone a clear picture: "Here's what you'd net via traditional listing over 6–9 months. Here's what cash nets you in 10 days. Here's what novation could net you. Here's what seller financing could net you over time." Hard to argue with specific numbers.

**Scenario: One sibling lives in the house and won't leave.**

Common when one adult child was caring for an elderly parent. Options:
- Buy out their share (we can structure the financing for this)
- Include a lease-back in the sale (they stay as renter post-sale)
- Partition action (court-ordered sale) as last resort

**Scenario: Heirs scattered across the country.**

We handle this 100% remotely. All signatures via DocuSign. All communication via video call with everyone joined from different cities. Common for Atlanta estates where heirs are in California, New York, Florida, etc.

**Scenario: Heirs need to close estate quickly for tax reasons.**

Some estates have timing issues — trust provisions, tax deadlines, surviving spouse needs. We can close within 10 days of probate completion, which keeps estate timing tight.
