## Tulsa's Current Market Reality

Drive through Tulsa, Broken Arrow, Jenks, Bixby, or Owasso right now and you'll see it: **"For Sale" signs up for months.** Reduced. Reduced again. Still no offers.

Specific to Tulsa's market:

- **Days-on-market has nearly doubled** since 2022. Homes that sold in 3 weeks now sit 60-120+ days.
- **Buyers are picky.** Any home that needs meaningful work struggles to find interest at all. Retail buyers want turnkey.
- **Local economy pressures.** Oil/gas industry volatility plus general inflation means Tulsa buyers are cautious.
- **Lots of 2020-2022 purchases now underwater.** Owners who bought at the peak with 6-7% mortgages often have little-to-no equity and can't absorb further price drops.
- **Traditional listings run 4-6+ months** with meaningful price reductions being the norm.
- **Many "as-is" homes never sell through MLS.** They sit, reduce, sit more, and eventually get delisted.

For sellers, this is frustrating. You're being asked to compete on both price AND condition in a way you weren't 3 years ago. If you can't bring your home to showroom-ready AND price below market, traditional listing is probably the wrong move.

## Why Tulsa Is Particularly Suited to Creative Finance

Here's something most Tulsa cash buyers won't tell you: **the structure of your sale matters more in Tulsa than in higher-priced markets.** Here's why:

**1. Lower equity per sale means cash offers leave less room.** Tulsa median home prices ($210-250k) mean sellers often have $40-80k in equity at most. A cash offer at 75% of retail doesn't leave much actual cash.

**2. More 2020-2022 buyers are underwater.** If you bought a Tulsa home in 2021 for $240k with 5% down, you paid roughly $228k cash-in. If the market value is now $215k (very realistic), you have zero or negative equity. **A cash offer doesn't work** — there's nothing to offer. **Subject-to DOES work** — we take over your mortgage payments and you walk away clean.

**3. Favorable mortgage rates worth preserving.** Lots of Tulsa homeowners have 3% loans from 2020-2021. That loan is genuinely worth money in a 7% rate environment. Subject-to preserves it.

**4. Retiree population wants income, not lump sums.** Tulsa has a significant retiree population who inherited homes or bought decades ago. Many would rather have $2,000/month for 15 years (seller financing) than $200k cash today.

**5. Our investor network pays more than local cash buyers.** We have hedge funds, family offices, and professional investors on our buyer list — entities with capital to deploy who don't care about the Tulsa "80% of retail" standard.
