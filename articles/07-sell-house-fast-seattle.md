# Selling Your Seattle Home in a Slow 2026 Market — Why Creative Finance Wins

**Even Seattle has cooled. The market that couldn't stop running in 2021 has significantly slowed in 2026. Homes that would have sold in a week are now sitting 60-120+ days. Tech layoffs, 7% mortgage rates, and buyer caution have all contributed. For Seattle sellers — where home values are high and each sale has six-figure tax implications — the structure of your sale has become dramatically more important. This guide explains what's actually working for Puget Sound sellers in today's market.**

## Seattle's Market Reality in 2026

The Puget Sound housing market has shifted meaningfully from its 2021-2022 peak:

- **Days-on-market nearly tripled** versus 2021. Even the desirable Eastside sees homes sitting 60-120+ days before offer.
- **Tech industry layoffs** (Amazon, Microsoft, Meta, Boeing) have reduced the high-income buyer pool
- **7% mortgage rates** mean buyers can afford 30-40% less house than they could at 3% rates
- **Price reductions common** — homes that listed at $950k often sell at $875k after 4 months
- **Tacoma and South Sound** see longer listings than Seattle proper — 90-150+ days is common
- **Inspection demands have gotten aggressive** — buyers routinely ask for $10-30k in concessions
- **"As-is" homes really struggle** — buyers have leverage and use it

**For Seattle sellers specifically, the stakes are higher:**

Seattle median home prices hover $820k-$950k. That means:
- More equity at stake per sale (six-figure decisions)
- Bigger capital gains tax bills (often $50-150k+)
- More sellers with favorable 2020-2022 mortgage rates worth preserving
- Bigger difference between the "right" and "wrong" structure

**In today's market, a typical Seattle seller can easily net $50,000-$200,000 more by choosing the right structure.**

### 3. Seller Financing (Often the BEST for Seattle Sellers in 2026)

**Who should strongly consider this:**

**Required: consult a CPA before signing.** The math is usually strongly in favor of seller financing for high-value Seattle sales, but every situation is different.

### 4. Subject-To (Preserves Your 2020-2022 Mortgage)

**Especially powerful in Seattle 2026.** Why?

If you have a Seattle mortgage from 2020-2022 with a 2.75-3.5% rate, that loan is literally worth money now. New mortgages are 6.5-7.5%. **A subject-to deal preserves that favorable rate.**

**Seattle example:**
- 4BR in Bellevue, $1.15M market value
- Your mortgage: $870k balance at 2.875% (2021 origination)
- Your equity: $280k
- Rather than selling for $900k cash (lost 2.875% rate), we:
  - Pay you $280k cash for your equity
  - Take over your $870k mortgage at 2.875%
  - **You walk away with $280k cash + mortgage off your credit**
  - Meanwhile, your 2.875% mortgage continues being paid on time

**Who this is for:**
- Seattle sellers with low-rate mortgages from 2020-2022
- Sellers with modest equity who can't absorb a cash sale discount
- Sellers who need out but don't want to trigger a large capital gains event

## Washington-Specific Considerations in 2026

### No State Income Tax

Simplifies things compared to California or Oregon. Federal capital gains still apply.

### Real Estate Excise Tax (REET)

Washington's REET applies to sellers at 1.1–3.0% depending on sale price. On a $900k Seattle home: $9,900-$27,000. **Who pays this is negotiating point in contracts** — we always confirm upfront.

### Earthquake/Seismic Disclosures

Seattle homes have required seismic disclosures. Retrofitted? Not? Disclose. We buy homes with known seismic risks or partial retrofits.

### Flood and Landslide Zones

Some Seattle neighborhoods (Green Lake, Magnolia, certain view properties) have flood or landslide risk. We buy these. Many conventional lenders won't finance them — an advantage for creative structures where bank financing isn't needed.

### Tech Industry Shifts

Amazon layoffs, Microsoft reorganization, Boeing restructuring — all these factors have reduced high-income buyer activity. Traditional listings depending on "tech buyer demand" are more vulnerable in 2026.
