⚠ Pasco County market reality

New Port Richey and Wesley Chapel sellers are dealing with a tough market.

Across Pasco County, homes are sitting longer than they have in years. Insurance costs have spiked dramatically — buyers factor this into every offer, and properties showing any wear are generating far fewer showings. Traditional listings routinely run 6–9 months with price reductions. New construction in Wesley Chapel and Land O'Lakes has given buyers leverage they didn't have two years ago.

Hurricane Helene and Milton left many Pasco County homes with damage that's expensive to fix and hard to disclose on a traditional listing. We buy storm-damaged and flood-affected homes as-is — no contractor required on your end.

We offer five structures: cash (10-day close), seller financing (monthly income), subject-to, wholesale, and novation. Veterans get an extra $250 at closing.

Pasco County FAQ

How quickly can you close on a Pasco County house?

We can close in as few as 10 days on a cash offer. If you need more time — probate, coordinating a move, or planning a relocation — we work on your schedule.

Do I need to repair or clean out the house before selling?

No. We buy Pasco County homes exactly as they are — storm damage, dated interiors, deferred maintenance, or any condition. No repairs, no cleaning, no staging required.

What areas of Pasco County do you buy in?

We buy throughout all of Pasco County: New Port Richey, Wesley Chapel, Land O'Lakes, Zephyrhills, Dade City, Port Richey, Holiday, Trinity, Hudson, and surrounding areas.

Can you work with a vacant or tenant-occupied rental?

Yes. We buy Pasco County properties with tenants in place or vacant. No need to wait for a lease to expire or coordinate an eviction first.

What if my house has flood or hurricane damage?

We buy storm-damaged and flood-affected homes throughout Pasco County. Helene and Milton left many properties in difficult-to-sell condition — we buy them as-is, no contractor needed on your end.
